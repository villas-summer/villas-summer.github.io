Chicken Adobo Recipe

Preparation Time: 10-15 Minutes

Cook Time: 30-45 minutes (Dependent on size of chicken)

About Adobo

Chicken Adobo is the ultimate Filipino comfort food—a mouthwatering blend of tender chicken simmered in a rich, tangy sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns. Infused with bold, savory flavors and a perfect balance of salty and sour, this iconic dish is a true taste of the Philippines. Whether you're new to Filipino cuisine or a seasoned fan, Chicken Adobo is a must-try dish that’s guaranteed to delight your taste buds. Serve it with steaming white rice to soak up that irresistible sauce, and you’ve got a meal that’s pure, delicious magic!

Ingrdient List












Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken
    • 1 kg bone-in chicken thighs
    • 1/2 cup soy sauce
    • 1/4 cup white vinegar
    • 5-6 cloves garlic, finely minced
    In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic. Add the chicken thighs and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sear the Chicken
    Heat cooking oil in a pan, sear the chicken until golden brown, then set aside.
  3. Soften the Aromatics
    • 1 medium onion, sliced (optional)
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
    In the same pan, cook the onions until soft, then add bay leaves and peppercorns for flavor.
  4. Add the Marinade and Simmer
    Pour the reserved marinade and cook for 2 minutes. Return the chicken to the pan and add water to cover halfway.
  5. Add Water and Simmer Gently
    • 1 cup water
    Simmer the chicken for 30 minutes or until tender.
  6. Reduce the Sauce
    Taste the sauce and adjust with salt or pepper. Let it reduce until thickened.
  7. Serve
    Serve the Chicken Adobo with steamed white rice and enjoy!

Recipe Notes

  • Marinating Time - For optimal flavour, we recommend marinating the chicken for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight. This will allow the marinade;s flavours to fully infuse the chicken.
  • Other Additions - The original recipe is delicious on its own, but some people like to add boiled eggs or potatoes for an extra touch.
  • Serving Suggestions - This recipe is traditionally served with white rice, which helps soak up and enjoy all the flavours. For added freshness, you can top it with green onions, or for a spicy kick, add thai chillies.
  • Explore Other Variations - Adobo can be made with other proteins, such as pork or seafood like shrimp and squid. adding coconut milk can introduce a rich, velvety tecture while preserving the savory and tangy flavours of the traditional dish
